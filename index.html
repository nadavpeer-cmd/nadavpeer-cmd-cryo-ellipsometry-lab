Ellipsometry & Physics Models

1. Polarization Change (Ψ & Δ)

An ellipsometer measures the complex reflectance ratio (ρ) between p- and s-polarized light. Ψ represents the amplitude ratio, and Δ is the phase shift.

ρ = rp / rs = tan(Ψ)eiΔ

2. Complex Refractive Index (n, k)

Using a layer-stack model, the raw Ψ and Δ are inverted to find the complex refractive index, dictating dispersion (n) and absorption (k).

N = n - ik

3. Absorption & Direct Bandgap (Tauc Relation)

The absorption coefficient (α) is calculated from k. For direct bandgap materials like GaAs, plotting (αhν)² reveals the optical bandgap (Eg) at the x-intercept.

α = 4πk / λ     |     (αhν)² = A(hν - Eg)

4. Temperature Dependence (Varshni)

Describes how the bandgap shrinks as temperature increases due to lattice expansion and electron-phonon interactions.

Eg(T) = Eg(0) - (αT²) / (T + β)
